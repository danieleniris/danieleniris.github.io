Is plasmonic nanotweezing of single proteins possible?

Optical tweezing, using light to manipulate small objects, has revolutionized molecular biophysics as it allowed the study of proteins, the molecular workhorses, at the single-molecule level. Plasmonic nanotweezers are posed to further revolutionize this technology, but even 13 years after their invention, they haven't delivered. Why is that?
